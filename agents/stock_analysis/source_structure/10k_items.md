# Business Overview & Risk
## Item 1. Business
**What it includes:** A description of the company’s main operations, including its products and services, subsidiaries, and markets.
**Key Details:** It typically covers competition, regulation, seasonality, labor issues, and special operating costs. This is the "elevator pitch" of what the company actually does.

## Item 1A. Risk Factors
**What it includes:** A list of the most significant risks that could negatively impact the company’s business, financial condition, or stock price.
**Key Details:** Risks are usually listed in order of importance. This includes market risks, regulatory risks, technological obsolescence, and specific operational liabilities.

## Item 1B. Unresolved Staff Comments
**What it includes:** This section is required if the company has received written comments from the SEC staff regarding its periodic reports that remain unresolved for more than 180 days.
**Key Details:** It explains why the company and the SEC have not yet reached an agreement on accounting or disclosure issues.

## Item 1C. Cybersecurity
**What it includes:** (Newer requirement) Disclosures regarding the company's cybersecurity risk management, strategy, and governance.
**Key Details:** Information on how the board oversees cyber risk and the management's role in assessing and managing material risks from cybersecurity threats.

## Item 2. Properties
**What it includes:** A description of the physical properties the company owns or leases.
**Key Details:** This outlines major factories, warehouses, office headquarters, and retail stores, including their locations and whether they are owned or leased.

## Item 3. Legal Proceedings
**What it includes:** A summary of significant pending lawsuits or other legal actions.
**Key Details:** It usually excludes ordinary routine litigation incidental to the business (e.g., a slip-and-fall at a grocery store) but includes major lawsuits that could materially affect finances.

## Item 4. Mine Safety Disclosures
**What it includes:** Safety information for mining companies.
**Key Details:** If the company operates mines, it must list violations or citations from the Mine Safety and Health Administration (MSHA).

# Financial Data & Management Analysis
## Item 5. Market for Registrant’s Common Equity, Related Stockholder Matters and Issuer Purchases of Equity Securities
**What it includes:** Information about the company’s stock.
**Key Details:** Ticker symbol, exchange listing, high/low stock prices, dividend history, and details on any share buybacks (repurchases) the company performed during the year.

## Item 6. [Reserved]
Note: This section previously contained "Selected Financial Data" (a 5-year summary), but the SEC removed this requirement in 2021 to simplify disclosures. It is now marked as "Reserved."

## Item 7. Management’s Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations (MD&A)
**What it includes:** Management’s narrative explanation of the financial results.
**Key Details:** This is often considered one of the most important sections. It explains why the numbers went up or down. Topics include liquidity, capital resources, and results of operations (revenue trends, margin changes).

## Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk
**What it includes:** Exposure to market risks.
**Key Details:** How interest rate changes, foreign currency exchange rates, and commodity price fluctuations might affect the bottom line.

## Item 8. Financial Statements and Supplementary Data
**What it includes:** The audited financial statements.
**Key Details:** Use the tool `list_statements` and `get_statement` to get a specific financial statement. Only get this item if that fails.

## Item 9. Changes in and Disagreements With Accountants on Accounting and Financial Disclosure
**What it includes:** Disclosure if the company fired its auditor or if the auditor resigned.

**Key Details:** It will detail any disagreements over accounting principles that led to the change.

## Item 9A. Controls and Procedures
**What it includes:** The CEO and CFO's assessment of the company's internal controls over financial reporting.

**Key Details:** This is a Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) requirement. They must state if their controls are effective at preventing fraud or errors.

## Item 9B. Other Information
**What it includes:** Any information that was required to be reported on a Form 8-K during the fourth quarter but wasn't.

## Item 9C. Disclosure Regarding Foreign Jurisdictions that Prevent Inspections
**What it includes:** Identifies if the company is using an auditor located in a foreign jurisdiction (often relevant for Chinese companies listed in the US) where the PCAOB is restricted from inspecting the audit work.

# Part III: Governance & Compensation
Information on who runs the company and how much they are paid. Companies often omit this section in the 10-K and instead refer readers to their Proxy Statement (Form DEF 14A), which is filed separately.

## Item 10. Directors, Executive Officers and Corporate Governance
**What it includes:** Names, ages, and backgrounds of directors and officers.
**Key Details:** Also includes the company’s code of ethics and the audit committee’s composition.

## Item 11. Executive Compensation
**What it includes:** Detailed tables showing how much top executives were paid.
**Key Details:** Salary, bonuses, stock options, and other perks.

## Item 12. Security Ownership of Certain Beneficial Owners and Management and Related Stockholder Matters
**What it includes:** Who owns the company's stock.
**Key Details:** Lists any individual or entity that owns more than 5% of the company, as well as the shares owned by directors and officers.

## Item 13. Certain Relationships and Related Transactions, and Director Independence
**What it includes:** "Related party" transactions.
**Key Details:** Discloses deals between the company and its executives/directors (e.g., if the company rents a building owned by the CEO's brother).

## Item 14. Principal Accountant Fees and Services
**What it includes:** Fees paid to the external auditor.
**Key Details:** Broken down by audit fees, tax fees, and other consulting fees.

# Part IV: Exhibits & Schedules
## Item 15. Exhibits, Financial Statement Schedules
**What it includes:** A list of all documents filed with the report.
**Key Details:** This includes the company’s Bylaws, Articles of Incorporation, material contracts (e.g., major employment agreements, massive debt agreements), and a list of subsidiaries.

## Item 16. Form 10-K Summary
**What it includes:** An optional summary of the information contained in the report.
**Key Details:** Usually a brief index or executive summary, though many companies choose not to include this.
